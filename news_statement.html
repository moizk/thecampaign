Chrissy's Statement on Stop, Question and Frisk Ruling

Chrissy Voskerichian, candidate for City Council in Northeast Queens, issued the following statement regarding the decision of Federal Judge Shira Scheindlin that the NYPD's use stop, question and frisk violates the constitutional rights of some of those who are stopped.

"This ruling will have a negative impact on the safety of the City" said Chrissy. "The Judge's ruling will have the same consequences as the Community Safety Act. It will prevent the NYPD from stopping individuals who match the description of dangerous suspects. The judge also appointed a monitor that will undoubtedly cost the City millions of dollars and circumvent the power of the Police Commissioner. The federal monitor will have the same impact as the Community Safety Act's Inspector General. The ruling is a serious set back to the ability of the NYPD to police proactively. It is needless to say that passing the Community Safety Act now would just create even further confusion and result in three different officials with oversight of the NYPD. Today is a bad day for the safety of New York City."
